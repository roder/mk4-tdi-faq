Introduction

This article shows how to remove the aluminum oil pan and install a hybrid steel/aluminum oil pan on a VW or Audi TDI engine.

NOTE: This article is for the mk4 Jetta, Golf, and New Beetle. For the B5 Passat, see 1000q: oil chain removal/oil pan removal - the front subframe has to be lowered and supported for clearance.

The aluminum oil pan is easily cracked if car bottoms out in a pothole, is hit by a rock, you run over a road alligator (shredded and blown out truck tires), or hits other road hazards. This article shows installation of a hybrid steel bottomed/aluminum oil pan that is more durable.

However, any good hit will crack the aluminum sides of the oil pan vs. a metal skidplate which transfers the impact to the body through the supports. To decrease the liklihood of cracking the oil pan, refresh the suspension if it's worn out (many years of weight sitting on the springs causes them to sag and the bushings to compress) and get a metal skidplate to replace the plastic water shield. Dieselgeek.com makes a good skidplate, refer to 1000q: metal skidplate installation for more tips and notes.

If you need to replace the front or rearl main seal, see 1000q: mk4 front seal replacement or 1000q: mk4 rear main seal replacement

Parts

10mm and various metric sockets
T-20 torx bit to remove belly shield/plastic cover
liquid gasketmaker for the oil pan
replacement aluminum oil pan from kermatdi.com VW# 038 103 601 na
steel-aluminum hybrid oil pan VW# 06a 103 601 t

2004+ models require M12 triple square 12 point bit

Procedure

First drain the oil: see 1000q: mk4 oil change for tips. If you spill some oil on the ground, first wipe it up with towels.  Then pour some driveway spill absorber or basic kitty litter on the spill.  Step and grind the litter into a dust and let it sit for a while.  It needs time to soak up the stain.  When it's saturated, sweep up the gravel/dust.  Do not dump old engine oil onto the ground or into the water!  If you can't find a dump for used coolant/antifreeze, engine oil, gear oil, or other car fluids, the website earth911.com can search for a local waste disposal.

If the car is not yet in the air, engage the parking brake, jack up the front of the car using the factory jack points, rest car securely on jack stands, chock the rear wheels, and make sure the car is safe and secure before doing anything else.  I use wood blocks that I made to help support the car but always place jack stands at the factory hack points before getting under the car.  See the TOS Agreement for the full legal disclaimer.  See 1000q: jack stand points to see where the points were on my car.

The following tips and pictures are from user Richard55, thank you for your contribution!

Remove the right wheel.

Remove the two A/C mounting bolts and pry the a/c pump toward the front of the car.


Next the three bellhousing bolts on the drivers side of the oil pan.

Note: 2004-2006 models use 12 point triple square spline bits but it's tight. The size is M12, you can find these at any autozone/napa/advance. A T55 torx (6 point) will also fit in an emergency but beware stripped bolts...


Break loose all the 10mm bolts that hold the pan on, don't for get the four hidden ones inside the bellhousing on the drivers side and the ones at the passengers end of the pan. I broke them all loose and got out the trusty drill with the 1/4" adaptor and go to town.


After they are all out, gently pry the pan off the block, slow and easy it will come off. Editors note: you can tap the pan with a rubber mallet or hammer and piece of wood to shock and loosen the seal. I do not suggest prying on the gasket surfaces with anything sharp.


It's off and here are the guts.


Chain, oil pump, and sprokets all in great shape.

Two 10mm bolts and R&I with a new oil pickup tube.


Clean off all the old RTV and grease/oil. Take your time and get it all off, and install the new pan. The bead of liquid gasketmaker should to the inner side of the boltholes to prevent oil leaks.



Check pan to a/c pump clearance and if good you are done. If not loosen the a/c pump and file off until you have enough clearance.
